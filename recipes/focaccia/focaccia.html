Focaccia

Description

Focaccia is a flat oven-baked Italian bread product similar in style and texture to pizza dough. Focaccia can be used as a side to many meals or as sandwich bread. Focaccia al rosmarino (focaccia with rosemary) is a common focaccia style in Italian cuisine that may be served as an antipasto, appetizer, table bread, or snack. It is similar in style and texture to pizza dough, and is typically leavened with yeast. It may be topped with herbs or other ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/3 cups warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon coarse sea salt

Steps

  1. Place water, sugar, salt, flour and yeast in the pan of the bread machine in the order recommended by the manufacturer. Select Dough cycle; press Start.
  2. Remove dough from pan after the dough cycle is complete. Pat dough into either a 9x13 inch pan or a 12 inch round pizza pan. Use your fingers to dimple the dough and drizzle with olive oil. Sprinkle with rosemary and sea salt.
  3. Let rise for 20 to 25 minutes.
  4. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
  5. Bake in preheated oven for 20 to 25 minutes, or until golden brown.
